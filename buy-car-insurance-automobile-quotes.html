How to compare car insurance

This unbiased guide — written by The GO Insurance's insurance experts — outlines the factors that comprise car insurance premiums and provides tips to help you compare car insurance quotes and find the right car insurance policy, whether it's for your personal vehicle or commercial vehicle insurance.

Key Takeaways

  • Car insurance rates, including third party car insurance, vary by person and by company. Who you pick as your insurance provider can be just as impactful as your driving record.
  • It is recommended to compare auto insurance quotes, including 3rd party insurance, from different companies every six months.

GO Insurance Tip: Price isn't everything – consider company reputation

Sometimes an insurance company is cheap for a reason. After an accident is not the time to wish you'd known about your provider's poor customer service and claims record. Before purchasing a policy, consult a company's ratings from trusted sources like The GO Insurances Satisfaction Survey.

Comparing car insurance companies and their services

Of the companies listed above, USAA is one of the cheapest but it's also the most exclusive, catering to military members and their families – so while the lower premium is appealing, not everyone will qualify. Although Allstate is more expensive, it does not have occupation restrictions and it's one of the best and largest insurance companies, providing comprehensive car insurance policies. For many customers, the peace of mind and availability of local agents is worth the additional cost. Outside of its services, a company might be more expensive because of simple economics. If your current company paid out more claims than it earned in premium, it could account for that by increasing rates next year.

Even if you didn't file a claim, your rate can still increase because your insurance company's cost of doing business increased.
